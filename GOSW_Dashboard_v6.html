Technology Status

Offshore wind has matured rapidly, with average turbine capacity growing from 3 MW in 2010 to over 10 MW in 2023. Floating wind remains nascent but is accelerating, with 244 GW in the development pipeline.

⚓
Monopile
Dominant foundation type. ~78% of European installations. Depth up to 40m.

Market, Policy & Finance

2024 was a record year for offshore wind auctions globally. Yet only 8 countries have embedded explicit offshore wind targets in their NDCs — and even the most optimistic 2030 forecasts fall 42% short of the capacity required by the IRENA 1.5°C pathway.

OSW Investment Data Gap
⚠️
OSW-specific investment data is not available from IRENA, GWEC, or ESMAP in a directly comparable, verified format. IRENA's Public Financial Flows cover all wind (on+offshore). IRENA WETO investment needs are for all renewables, not OSW in isolation. A verified OSW-only global investment figure requires BloombergNEF or CPI data — outside the permitted source set for this dashboard.
For context, the IEA projects OSW will need ~$52 billion in annual global investment through 2030 to meet net-zero targets (IEA Net Zero by 2050, 2021). This figure is not from a permitted source and is not displayed as dashboard data.
⚠️
Data note — Finance: IRENA's Public Financial Flows Dashboard covers the combined wind sector (onshore + offshore). OSW-specific public investment flows are not disaggregated in IRENA data. Private investment data (BloombergNEF, CPI) is also excluded from this source-filtered version.
